About me

I am Almudena Montiel González. I am originally from beautiful Sevilla, in the south of Spain. I graduated at the University of Sevilla in 2007, and afterwards I got my MSc. in Grid Computing in Cranfield University in 2008, funded by the Erasmus Program. I kicked into gear with a Leonardo grant in Amsterdam, I had a great time, and also worked, for M2MOBI, a fresh and brand new company developing innovative and ingenious mobile phone applications. I worked in the systems side, improving the performance of the servers through redesigning of the databases and servers architecture. One of those great days, I received a call that I was given a grant which I asked for some time ago. This way, in September 2009, I started to work at GSI in the Scientific Computing Group lead by Peter Malzacher. Here I have met great profesionals, despite their young age. You can check my blog to see more of what I have done/I am doing during this time. I am slowly approaching my third year here, but don't assume I am able to speak German because of that fact (Ich brauche nur noch sieben Leben mehr).
At the moment my project is financed by the CRISP project, which is inside the ESFRI roadmap.

In my free time I practice sport, only amateur. I go swimming to the Nordbad in Darmstadt, I go jogging, I play football with my friends, and whenever I have the chance I go running races around Darmstadt. This is the best website if you are interested in such races: Lauftreff.

I enjoy very much travelling, especially when I have the chance to discover new landscapes, foods and lifestyles.
